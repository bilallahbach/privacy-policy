## Summary
LaBDrop transfers files over your local WiFi network between phones and computers. It does **not** upload your files to our servers, does **not** create user accounts, and does **not** use ads or trackers for advertising.

## What the app does
LaBDrop can:
- Receive files from a PC browser or another phone on the same WiFi network
- Send files to another phone running LaBDrop
- Share selected files so you can download them from a PC browser on the same network

Transfers happen directly between devices on your local network. The developer does not operate a cloud file-storage service for this app.

## Data we collect
We do **not** collect, sell, or share your personal data through LaBDrop servers, because LaBDrop does not use a backend account/cloud service operated by us.

### Data that stays on your devices / local network
- Files you choose to send or receive
- Temporary session information such as a local PIN, session token, device name, and local IP/port used for discovery and transfer
- Files saved on your phone (for example in Downloads/LaBDrop)

This information is used only to perform the transfer you started. It is not sent to the developer for analytics advertising profiles.

## Permissions
Depending on your Android version, LaBDrop may request permissions such as:
- Network / WiFi related access — to discover nearby devices and transfer files on your local network
- Notifications — to show when the app is ready to receive files
- Storage / media-related access (as required by Android) — to save received files and let you pick files to send

These permissions are used only for the app’s file-transfer features.

## Local web page for PC transfers
When Receive mode is on, the phone can show a local link/QR code. Opening that link on a computer on the same WiFi displays a simple local page hosted by your phone so you can upload or download files. That page is served from your phone on the local network and is protected by a session token/PIN for that session.

## Children
LaBDrop is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

## Third-party services
LaBDrop’s core transfer features do not require Google sign-in, social login, ads SDKs, or cloud storage SDKs operated by the developer.

If you install LaBDrop from Google Play, Google may process data according to Google’s own policies (for example Play distribution and crash/statistics if enabled by the platform).

## Data security
Transfers use your local network. Anyone on the same network who obtains your active session link/token or PIN during an active receive session could potentially send or access shared files for that session. Turn Receive mode off when you are finished. Use trusted WiFi networks.

## Changes
We may update this policy when the app changes. The “Last updated” date will be revised when we do.
